Protecting Our Future

Every citizen plays a vital role in identifying and protecting exploited and missing children. Learn the signs and take action.

Why Community Education Matters

Children who are exploited or missing are often hidden in plain sight. By educating ourselves on the warning signs, we can intervene early and save lives. A vigilant community is a child's strongest safety net.

Recognizing the Warning Signs

Know what to look for in a child who might be in danger.

Physical Signs

  • Unexplained bruises, burns, or injuries.
  • Poor hygiene or inappropriate dress for the weather.
  • Signs of malnutrition or untreated medical conditions.
  • Fatigue or falling asleep in school/public.

Behavioral Signs

  • Sudden changes in behavior or school performance.
  • Extreme fear of going home or of specific adults.
  • Withdrawal from friends, family, or activities.
  • Appearing fearful, anxious, or depressed.

Missing Children

  • Children seen wandering alone in unsafe areas.
  • A child who seems lost but is afraid to ask for help.
  • An adult controlling a child who seems distressed.
  • A child who does not know their address or parents' names.

What To Do If You Suspect Abuse

Your actions can save a life. Follow these steps.

1

Observe and Listen

Pay attention to what the child says and how they act. Do not interrogate them, but listen supportively if they speak.

2

Ensure Immediate Safety

If the child is in immediate physical danger (e.g., a lost child on a highway), secure their safety first, but avoid putting yourself or them in further danger.

3

Report Immediately

Contact the Police, the Child Protection Hotline, or a Social Welfare Officer. Do not wait. Reporting anonymously is an option if you fear retaliation.

4

Document Details

Note down descriptions of people involved, locations, vehicle license plates, or any other relevant information before you forget.

Never Confront the Abuser

If you suspect a child is being exploited by an adult, do not confront the adult directly. This could escalate the danger for the child. Report to authorities instead.

Need Help? Call Now

If you see a child in danger, call the National Child Helpline.
